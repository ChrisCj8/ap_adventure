<p>The Region Tab is where you set up your maps regions.</p>
<p>Regions are containers for access conditions that Archipelago uses to help make designing logic easier, they are especially useful for apworlds that implement entrance randomization, such as this one. They are used to group locations with similar access conditions together and do not have to map to physical space.</p>
<p>Regional Conditions are a system unique to apAdventure that tell the generator what items are logically useable in a certain region. It was originally created for and is mainly used to mark what ammunition is available to the player so they won't be expected to get through an area with a weapon that they may not be able to find any ammunition for.</p>
<p>When setting Ammo Conditions, regions shouldn't be marked as containing a certain ammo type if there's not enough of it to be useful in a fight. For example, it's a good idea not to mark a region as containing rockets just because it has a single RPG round, as the player could easily end up wasting it. In general, it's usually better to have logic be too strict than not strict enough so the player doesn't end up with an unbeatable or frustratingly difficult run, as long it's not so strict that it ends up causing generation failures.</p>
<p>One thing that should also be kept in mind when marking ammo availability is that Half-Life 2 features dynamic item spawns, so crates and pickup spots might change their contents depending on how much ammo, health and/or armor the player has or be empty/absent entirely. Ammo pickups also grant less ammo at higher difficulties. You can always place some extra ammo manually if you think ammo availability may be too tight.</p>
